LESSONS LEARNED — AFTER ACTION
L1: TMD Inventory is the Strategic Constraint
The 82-missile Iranian salvo consumed 60% of forward-deployed PAC-3 interceptors in a single engagement. With Iran's estimated 3,000+ missile inventory, the campaign TMD shortfall is existential. Investment priority: NEXT-GEN INTERCEPTOR and forward pre-positioned SM-6 stocks. Consider directed energy supplements.
L2: Hormuz Mining Was Underplanned
IRGCN mine-laying began simultaneously with H-Hour, suggesting pre-positioned assets outside our ISR coverage. MCM assets were not forward-staged. 12 commercial vessels struck within 48 hours. Lessons: persistent MH-60S MCM helicopter detachments must be pre-deployed with CSG; LCS MCM mission module to theater at D-14.
L3: Proxy Networks Require Independent Suppression Operations
The assumption that kinetic degradation of IRGC HQ would interrupt Hezbollah and Houthi activation proved incorrect. These networks operated with full autonomy under pre-established execution orders. Recommendation: simultaneous Phase 0 operations against Hezbollah C2 and Houthi missile assets prior to main effort.
